Every inbound applicant is screened automatically when they apply. There's nothing to configure beyond the [job's hiring criteria](/jobs#writing-good-criteria).

## What the agent does

It reads the CV and the answers to your application questions, then evaluates the candidate against each criterion on the job. The result is a per-criterion verdict with a short explanation grounded in the candidate's actual background.

<Info>
  Screening runs on every inbound source: [career page](/career-pages) applications, [forwarded emails](/email-forwarding), and manually added applicants.
</Info>

## Where to see results

Open the job's **Review** view — each applicant is listed with their assessment attached. The same assessment also lives on the candidate's [profile timeline](/pipeline#the-timeline).

## Acting on results

From the Review view, advance an applicant into the pipeline or reject them.

<Warning>
  Screening never rejects anyone on its own — it's input for your decision. The full CV and application answers are always one click away.
</Warning>

You can also handle screening from [Slack](/slack) ("show me the top applicants for backend engineer") or [MCP](/mcp-server) ("move the strongest phone-screen candidates to onsite").

## Improving results

Screening quality tracks criteria quality. If assessments keep missing something you care about, that's usually a sign the criterion isn't written down — add it to the job and new applicants are evaluated against the updated version. See [writing good criteria](/jobs#writing-good-criteria).
